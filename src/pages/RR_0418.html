Using video to describe your research

Karen Warkentin, professor of biology, and James Bird, assistant professor of mechanical engineering and materials science and engineering, shared their own experiences and strategies during the Storytelling through Video panel.

In a March 22 presentation, faculty members joined communications professionals from BU and MIT in a workshop that examined what makes a good visual story, how to distill a message, and how to use video to promote research. Watch the video.

Related: BU’s Strategic Communications Series wrapped up this semester with a discussion on how faculty and higher education representatives can “provide clear, relevant information to help shape policy or advocate for scientific research, the humanities, or for student financial aid.”
Varada named new ASC director

Dr. Rao Varada, who has served as the interim director of the Animal Science Center since September, was named as the new director and attending veterinarian.

NSF seeks input on reporting rules for assault or harrassment

The National Science Foundation is seeking feedback on new award requirements regarding the reporting of sexual and other forms of harassment and sexual assault, which is part of a larger effort to ensure safe research environments.

The Charles River Campus Institutional Review Board has updated the consent form for the use of video and other recordings obtained during a research study.
