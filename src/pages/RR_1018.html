The Research Revealed newsletter brings you behind the scenes so you can make the most of the vast research operations support network at BU. Please submit your newsletter ideas, and visit the Research Support website for more information.

Sponsored Programs and the Office of Research are now accepting nominations for this year’s Excellence in Research Administration Award, which honors two staff members — one in central administration and the other at a school or center — for exceptional service to the University's research mission.

Two new Affinity Research Collaboratives — one on tobacco regulatory science and the other on fibrosis in pathology — have been established to promote team science.

The BU School of Medicine is considering expanding the Metabolic Phenotyping and IVIS® Imaging cores and is conducting a survey to assess faculty members’ needs for mouse vascular or cardiovascular phenotyping.

The Office of Research is seeking faculty input on what types of topics would be most helpful for University researchers to explore in the Strategic Communications Series.

Pre-proposals for the Johnson & Johnson Innovation Lung Cancer Center pilot projects are due on Sept. 7.

Nominations for the BU Innovator of the Year Award are due on Sept. 14.

Applications for the Social Innovation on Drug Resistance (SIDR) Postdoctoral Program are due on Sept. 28.

Fallou Ngom, a CAS professor of anthropology and the director of the African Studies Center at the Pardee School of Global Studies, won a $250k collaborative research grant from the National Endowment for the Humanities for a project that will research and prepare for online and print publication texts written in West African languages that use Arabic script.

Federal funding for BU’s African Studies Center was also recently renewed by the U.S. Department of Education, which awarded more than $550k in continued support for both the center and its Foreign Language Area Studies fellowship program.

The Nanotechnology Innovation Center has awarded seed funding to projects led by Chen Yang (ENG), Xue Han (ENG), Xin Zhang (ENG), and Stephan Anderson (MED).

The Pardee Center for the Study of the Longer Range Future published a conference report stemming from its Symposium on Global Health & the Social Sciences.

The Initiative on Cities released Leading the American City, a five-part video history of mayoral leadership in the United States.

The Danielsen Institute has been awarded the Templeton Flourishing Grant for clinical and training research related to mental health care.

Shereen Shermak has been named the new executive director of the Hariri Institute-affiliated Boston Women’s Workforce Council.

New per diem animal rates have been announced.

The federal Institute of Education Sciences has made available various materials, including transcripts, Powerpoint presentations, and on-demand video webinars, to help researchers apply for funding opportunities.

Post Award Financial Operations has created a new onboarding pack for departments and updated its training video series with an entry on salary accrual for faculty on nine-month appointments.

Registrations for the 2018 a2ru conference are now open.
