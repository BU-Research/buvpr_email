Faculty share insights on communicating research through social media

Tammy Vigil, assistant professor in the College of Communication, and Joshua Safer, associate professor of medicine at the School of Medicine, were among the faculty sharing social media insights.

In a Feb. 7 panel presentation, faculty members who have participated in Reddit AMAs and Facebook Live events discussed why they decided to venture into social media, how they engaged their audiences, and the value they derived from it. Watch the video.
2018 Ignition Award recipients announced

Technology Development is pleased to announce the six winners of the 2018 Ignition Awards, which are designed to validate early-stage projects with clear commercial and medical potential. This year’s awards go to promising projects in fields ranging from immuno-oncology to medical imaging and software-based radio communications. Meet the winners.

Proud of your science or engineering-related visualization?

Nominate your work for the National Science Foundation’s Vizzie Awards! Now in its 16th year, these awards celebrate the universal language of imagery and its ability to exchange knowledge and increase understanding of scientific ideas and phenomena. Deadline is April 15. Learn more.

New IACUC software coming to CRC and MED campuses

The Institutional Animal Care & Use Committee will soon implement a new electronic protocol management system, Huron Click, to replace INSPIR (Medical Campus) and paper (Charles River Campus) protocols. The software is now in development and is expected to go live in the coming months. Learn more.

The next Coffee with Colleagues workshop will address the topic of effort. Submit your questions in advance.

The National Institutes of Health has increased the salary cap to $189,600, which should be used for applicable proposals and awards going forward. Read the NIH notice.

The Initiative on Cities is soliciting proposals for its Early Stage Urban Research Awards, which support research addressing urban challenges and urban populations in the United States and abroad. Deadline is March 25, 2018. Learn more.

The Genome Science Institute is requesting applications for its new seed funding program, which will award two one-year grants — ranging up to $20,000 — for proposals that enhance genetics and genomics research at the University or at Boston Medical Center. Deadline is April 10, 2018. Learn more.

The Digital Health Initiative is seeking research proposals that examine how technologies and computing/data sciences methodologies tackle problems in healthcare systems. Deadline is April 13, 2018. Learn more.
 

Completed and signed Personnel Activity Reports (PARs) are due on March 30.
Annual Postdoctoral Scholars Retreat
March 22-23, Charles River and Medical Campuses

Postdocs are invited to attend the Annual Postdoc Retreat, a two-day event spanning both campuses with skill development and networking opportunities tailored to your needs and interests. Drop in for a session or join for the whole time, whichever your schedule allows. Visit the Research website for a full agenda and to RSVP.
