Global Development Policy Center hosts international finance leaders in DC

The panel was moderated by GDP Center Director and Professor of Global Development Policy Kevin Gallagher, pictured left. Photo by Scavone Photography.

The Global Development Policy Center and BU Federal Relations hosted a panel discussion on April 13 featuring Jin Liqun, president of the Asian Infrastructure Investment Bank, and José Antonio Ocampo, co-chair of the Central Bank of Colombia. Titled "Brighter Prospects or Rose Colored Glasses? New Views on the Global Economy," the event coincided with meetings of the World Bank and the International Monetary Fund.

SPH professor Michael Siegel discusses gun violence in FB Live event

Before taking audience questions, Siegel described his research into state firearm laws and their effectiveness. Watch the video.
Nominate an early-career biomedicine researcher for STAT Wunderkinds

STAT is seeking nominations for its annual recognition of postdocs, medical residents, interns, medical fellows, and industry scientists. Qualified nominees have finished their terminal degrees and are in their first job/training program. Deadline for nominations is June 30.

The BU Nanotechnology Innovation Center is seeking proposals for projects focused on nanotechnology, nanobiotechnology, nanofabrication, etc. The amount of available funding ranges from $50-$100k per project and can be used for student/postdoctoral support, supplies, and equipment. Application deadline is June 1.

Dr. David Henderson, chair of psychiatry at the School of Medicine, won a $30,000 seed grant from BU Global Programs and BU Foundation–India for research that will study pathways to homelessness among people with serious mental illness in India and Boston.

Beta Bionics, a company founded and led by BU Professor of Biomedical Engineering Ed Damiano, received approval from the Food and Drug Administration to begin recruiting participants for a home-use study of its iLet bionic pancreas system. The bionic pancreas is driven by mathematical dosing algorithms developed in Damiano's BU lab.

The Charles River Campus IRB has updated its policy on recruitment and advertising to offer more flexibility when recruiting BU student and employee research participants.

New IACUC software that will bring protocol management at both the Medical and Charles River Campuses into one system is scheduled for a June launch.

Research Information Management System (RIMS) will undergo an upgrade June 4.

Radiation Safety has begun the process of migrating radioisotope use (RAM) permits to BioRAFT.
 

In anticipation of the high volume of proposal submissions for the fast approaching NIH summer deadlines, Sponsored Programs is asking that researchers notify the office of their intent to submit a proposal and adhere to the University’s Proposal Submission Policy.
 

Researchers planning to host summer students should ensure that they complete lab safety training and secure medical clearance from the Research Occupational Health Program.
